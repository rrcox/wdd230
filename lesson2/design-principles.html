Hick's Law

Google
https://www.google.com

Google's home page is the classic example of website making it easy for the user to do one thing - search. The number of stimuli is very small and therefore decision-making and getting results is very straightforward. There are virtually no significant options on the page but to enter text in the search box.

White Space and Clean Design

Apple
https://www.apple.com

Apple's home page uses very little text (except for some temporary announcements on the landing page) and lots of white space with large images on their pages. It make it easier for customers to focus on the design of there products visually.

PARC: Repetition

Tesla
https://www.tesla.com

On Tesla's home page repetition is used very effectively. The same typography throughout. The navigation bar does not move when scrolling. Images of the vehicles are all centered and have similar quality, lighting, and background giving each a consistent look.
